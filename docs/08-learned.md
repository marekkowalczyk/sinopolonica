## Governing Principle

Every semantic morpheme in a Latinate or Greek compound receives its own
character from the learned (Classical Chinese) register per P05. No Latin
or Greek stems remain in the script — only Polish grammatical endings
stay in Latin script.

The key test (P05): the suffix *-graf* is not a Polish grammatical
ending — it is a Greek root meaning "writer/recorder." It gets a
character. The suffix *-ia* on *kardiomiopatia* is a Polish grammatical
adaptation of a Greek ending — it stays in Latin script.

## Decomposition Method

1. Identify all semantic morphemes in the Latinate/Greek compound.
2. Assign each a character from the learned register.
3. Any remaining material is a Polish grammatical adaptation — leave it
   in Latin script.
4. If the compound has no Polish grammatical ending, the result is
   written entirely in characters.

## Register Interaction

When a Polish expression has both a Slavic and a Latinate form, the
choice of register is encoded in the character:

| Context | Expression | Rendering |
|---|---|---|
| Colloquial | choroba serca | 病 心a (everyday chars) |
| Medical report | kardiomiopatia | 衷筋病ia (learned chars) |

A reader seeing 衷 immediately knows they are in medical/technical
territory without needing any other context clue.
