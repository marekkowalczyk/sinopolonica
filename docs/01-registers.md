## Overview

The system draws characters from two pools. The choice of pool is itself
a semantic signal — it tells the reader whether the underlying Polish
morpheme is native Slavic or a learned borrowing.

## Everyday Register

Source: common kanji shared with Japanese jōyō set, or high-frequency
Classical Chinese characters.

Visual profile: relatively simple, low stroke count, widely recognized
across East Asian scripts.

Used for: native Slavic stems, core vocabulary, colloquial speech.

Examples: 心 (serce), 水 (woda), 目 (oko), 火 (ogień), 学 (nauka),
車 (wóz), 家 (dom), 犬 (pies), 林 (las).

## Learned Register

Source: rare or archaic Classical Chinese characters, often with higher
stroke counts and lower frequency in modern CJK usage.

Visual profile: complex, visually dense, unfamiliar to casual readers.

Used for: Latinate and Greek borrowings, medical/scientific terminology,
legal and academic language, formal synonyms of Slavic words.

Examples: 衷 (kardio-), 筋 (myo-), 齒 (denticja), 學 (edukacja),
軀 (korpus), 燧 (pyro-).

## Register Doublets

Many concepts have both a Slavic and a Latinate expression in Polish.
The system encodes this distinction at the character level per P07:

| Everyday (Slavic) | Learned (Latinate) | Polish pair |
|---|---|---|
| 心 | 衷 | serce / kardio- |
| 目 | 眸 | oko / okular- |
| 星 | 曜 | gwiazda / astro- |
| 火 | 燧 | ogień / pyro- |
| 水 | 潤 | woda / hydro- |
| 学 | 學 | nauka / edukacja |
| 体 | 軀 | ciało / korpus |
| 気 | 氣 | duch / psyche |
| 歯 | 齒 | ząb / denticja |

## Disambiguation Rule

When a reader encounters a character they do not recognize, the visual
complexity itself is informative: it signals learned register. A reader
fluent in everyday register can navigate most texts; encountering a
Classical character alerts them that they are in specialized territory.

This is structurally parallel to a Polish speaker encountering
*kardiomiopatia* vs. *choroba serca* — the Latinate form signals a
technical register even before the meaning is fully parsed.
